Most of my shower time is spent thinking about why things work and how that connects to a mathematical model. Emily gets exasperated. I've learned to save it for the blog.

I'm Josh — process engineer, compulsive modeler, father of two. I spend my days building statistical models for pharmaceutical manufacturing, which mostly means staring at data until it confesses something useful. Before that, injection molding and extrusion — different materials, same instinct: the process is always telling you something. Then I come home, and the habit doesn't stop.

I have a 3D printer, a coffee setup with too many variables, and two kids who have not yet asked me to stop explaining things. When that changes, I'll pivot.

This blog is what happens when the models escape the day job — documented in real time, with math, and with graphics. I don't have the answers before I start writing. That's the point.
